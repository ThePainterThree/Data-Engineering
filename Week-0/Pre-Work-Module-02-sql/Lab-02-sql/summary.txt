Lab M2.02 Summary

In this lab, I created and queried a multi-table database using customers, orders, products, order_items, and categories.

I used INNER JOIN to return only matching records between related tables, such as customers with orders and products with categories. 
I used LEFT JOIN when I needed to include all records from one table, even if there was no match, such as customers with zero orders. 
Since SQLite does not directly support RIGHT JOIN or FULL OUTER JOIN, I simulated them using reversed LEFT JOINs and UNION.

I used COUNT, SUM, AVG, MIN, and MAX to calculate totals. 

I used GROUP BY to summarize data by customer, product, category, country, and order status. 
I used HAVING to filter grouped results, such as customers with more than two orders or categories with revenue greater than 1000.

Challenges:
The most challenging parts were:
1.Know when to use WHERE versus HAVING;
2.How GROUP BY works when using multiple columns;
3.THe complex queries, breaking it down step by step, and (try) not to get confused in the process.
